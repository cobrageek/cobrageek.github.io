Warming Heads & Hearts

Our Girl Scout Troop is in Pasco County, Florida. The troop members started thinking about project ideas last November 2017. Many ideas were thought through and were personal to the members. While the troop was in the development stage we visited the Messengers of Hope Mission. The girls were won over by the focus on people in need within our county. In addition, the stories of people, especially the children who have gone without hurt their hearts. Each girl scout, left and knew that day that the bronze project had been refocused. They wanted to work to help people in their county not be cold and have to choose between food and warmth.

Here are some hats we have made using different types of yarn we found at local craft stores.

Step 1: What You Will Need

  • Orange round knitting loom (this will fit children and most Adults comfortably)
  • knitting hook
  • yarn needle
  • Ruler or Measuring tape
  • 1 skein fluffy yarn or 2 skeins non-fluffy yarn

Step 2: Yarn

We discovered using one strand of fluffy thread, or two strands of non-fluffy yarn. Using one fluffy or 2 non-fluffy strands will give you a great hat.

Step 3: Attach a Slip Knot on the Side Peg

Pull the yarn end out of your skein and make a slipknot near the end. (You don't need much of a tail at all) The tail will be hidden later. Loop this onto the horizontal loom peg and pull so that it is not too tight, but not too loose.

Step 4: Begin Looming

Bring the yarn behind the first vertical peg (to the left or right of the anchor peg) and then around the front. Continue the wrapping all the way around. Don't wrap too hard, because it will make your stitches hard to work with. Once you have all the pegs wrapped once, wrap them a second time. Now you should have two loops on each peg.

Step 5: Knit Your First Row

Take the knitting hook and begin at the first peg you wrapped. Push the hook under the bottom loop of yarn(s) and pull it over the top of the peg. That is the first stitch, continue the same movement of yarn for the remainder of the loop. To continue your knitting, you will want to wrap all the pegs again, so you have two loops of yarn on each peg and then bring the bottom yarn loop over the top of the peg. Wrapping and looping the yarn over the tops of each peg. Continue this until the knitted area reached around 4-5 inches in length.

Step 6: Knitting the Brim

Now that your knitted area is within 4-5 inches, we will start working on the brim. First, set the loom down on a flat surface peg side up. Make sure your working yarn comes through where it needs to be to keep knitting without any big gaps. Fold your knitting up through the middle of the loom. Also, make sure the anchor slip knot will be hidden within the folded area. Feel along the very beginning of the knitting; at the edge there are large open loops. These loops are to be placed on each peg, and will act as a loop of the yarn. Use the hook and lift the bottom hook over the top stich to secure the brim.

Step 9: Keep Knitting

Continue looping and stitching on each peg. As we have worked on our project we have found that 7 inches from brim to top is a good length for children, 8” for teenagers and 9” for Adults.

So, continue stitching until you reach your desired length.

Step 10:How To Remove the Hat From the Loom

Wrapp the yarn around the loom once, this will make sure there is enough yarn to finish the hat. Cut the yarn from the skein. Attache the yarn needle. Insert the needle, from the top of the peg and then pull yarn through. Continue all the way around the loom. As the stitching progresses, use the hook to remove the loops off the pegs. Once it is all stitched, it looks like a draw string bag. Pull tightly and then push the needle through a few stitches at the top and then inside. Gently, make a tight knot.

Step 11: Embellishments

This is where you can make additional items, buy them or decide to leave your hats just the way they are!
